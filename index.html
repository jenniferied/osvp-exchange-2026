About

Two continents, one volume

OSVP EXCHANGE brings together researchers, educators and practitioners working with LED volumes and real-time production pipelines, across an Asia–Europe axis. Building on the link between NTU Singapore (Cinematic Arts / XR Studio / CAVR), TH OWL (Virtual Film Playground), Film University Babelsberg (ProSys / On-Set Virtual Production) and HSHL (LED stage / VFX lab), the symposium pairs Europe's strength in educational and VP research with the technological and production momentum of the Asia-Pacific region. We invite contributions on production support systems, LED volume and display technology, real-time worldbuilding, tracking and stage elements, and access to Virtual Production in education.

Template. All submissions use the OSVP EXCHANGE template (download below), a single-column, two-column-compatible layout that matches the ACM acmart 'sigconf' proceedings format so that, if the venue is confirmed for ICPS, the accepted version converts to the ACM Digital Library through ACM TAPS without reformatting. Language. English

Peer review. Papers receive at least two documented, double-blind reviews. Anonymize submissions by removing author names and affiliations, using third-person self-citation and no grant numbers. Practice showcases that name a specific stage are reviewed single-blind where anonymization is not feasible.

Proceedings. We plan to publish every accepted contribution — paper, talk and workshop — with its own DOI. Publication in the ACM Digital Library (ICPS) is planned and subject to ACM approving the venue.*

* The proceedings are planned for the ACM Digital Library via the ACM International Conference Proceedings Series (ICPS). That application is in progress; until ACM approves the venue we cannot use the ACM name, the ICPS name or the ACM logo as a confirmed fact, so anything marked * is our intended route. If ICPS is not confirmed, accepted and peer-reviewed papers will appear in our own citable online proceedings with DOIs (e.g. via Zenodo). Either way the work is peer-reviewed and gets a DOI.

Important Dates

Everything below is worked backward from the provisional symposium date and shown as a range: the earliest a step can realistically happen, and the last day it can happen before the next step breaks. The binding constraint is the ICPS publication application. ACM needs it at least 60 days before the submission deadline and takes 4–6 weeks to decide, with no expedited review. The application can go out any time from now until about 10 July 2026 — but the longer we wait, the shorter the paper-review window becomes (apply at the last moment and review shrinks to ~2 weeks; apply by late June and a fuller ~4-week review stays possible).
